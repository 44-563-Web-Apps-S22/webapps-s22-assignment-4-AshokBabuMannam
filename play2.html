The Miser

Act 1
SCENE I
VALÈRE, ÉLISE

ELI.

Why do you not try also to gain my brother's goodwill,

in case the servant should betray our secret?

VAL.

I am afraid I cannot humour them both.

The temper of the father is so different from that of the son that it would be difficult to be the confidant of both at the same time. Rather try your brother yourself; make use of the love that exists between you to enlist him in our cause. I leave you, for I see him coming. Speak to him, sound him, and see how far we can trust him.

ELI.

I greatly fear

I shall never have the courage to speak to him of my secret
